Ken Thompson

Co-creator of UNIX, Turing Award laureate

Ken Thompson (seated) works with Dennis Ritchie in the 1970's

Here's an overview of Ken's life until now:

  • 1943 - Born in New Orleans, Louisiana
  • 1965 - Graduates with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from UC Berkeley
  • 1966 - Completes Berkeley's Masters program in EE&CS
  • 1966 - Takes a job at Bell Laboratories, where much of his famous work would originate
  • Early 1970's - Begins work on early versions of UNIX, assisted by Ritchie and other colleagues at Bell.
  • 1975 - Takes a break from his job to continue research in UNIX at Berkeley
  • Late 1970's - Creates Belle, one of the first computer chess players. Belle would go on to be the computer chess master for several years in the 1980's
  • 1983 - Receives the Turing Award for the creation of UNIX. Demonstrates how vulnerable computer software can be in his acceptance speech by planting a backdoor in a research version of the operating system
  • 1992 - Develops UTF-8 encoding scheme with Rob Pike. This encoding came to be the main encoding for the web
  • 1999 - Receives the National Medal of Technology along with Ritchie from President Clinton for the creation of UNIX and the C programming language
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the Indian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population
  • 2000 - Retires from Bell Labs
  • 2006 - Works at Google as a Distinguished Engineer, contributing to several recent projects such as the Go Language

Obviously, the peron who had the most influence on my career was Ken Thompson.

- Dennis Ritchie, creator of the C programming language
